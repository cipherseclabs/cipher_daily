Weekly Brief – July 10, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • Arrests of four suspected hackers linked to ransomware disruptions at UK retailers M&S, Co‑op, and Harrods – M&S lost ~£300 million and IT service was offline ~6 weeks. Incident ties to DragonForce and Scattered Spider gang tactics involving SIM‑swap and social engineering. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2025/jul/10/four-arrested-over-cyber-attacks-marks-and-spencer-co-op-harrods
  • Qantas breach impacted ~6 million Australian customers via offshore call‑center vishing attack; data included personal info but not payment credentials. Attack methods consistent with Scattered Spider using voice‑cloning. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/jul/06/qantas-attack-reveals-one-phone-call-is-all-it-takes-to-crack-cybersecuritys-weakest-link-humans
  • Ingram Micro ransomware (SafePay) disrupted internal operations and ordering platforms. Source: https://www.axios.com/newsletters/axios-future-of-cybersecurity-fab0c0d0-5760-11f0-8c09-e72f7ef7d797

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Microsoft released July Patch Tuesday: fixes for 128–137 CVEs including critical RCE in SPNEGO (CVE‑2025‑47981, CVSS 9.8), SQL Server zero‑day (CVE‑2025‑49719), SharePoint, Office flaws. Urgent patching advised. Source: https://www.securityweek.com/microsoft-patches-130-vulnerabilities-for-july-2025-patch-tuesday/
  • SAP July patches address 27 vulnerabilities, 7 critical across enterprise portfolio. Source: https://gbhackers.com/sap-july-2025-patch-day/

Geopolitical & Political Developments

  • UK signals intent to impose mandatory reporting on major cyberattacks after unreported incidents disclosed by M&S chairman. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/business/live/2025/jul/08/stock-markets-trump-tariff-letters-august-deadline-obr-uk-public-finances-marks-spencer-cyber-hack-business-live-news-updates

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • Chrome zero‑day CVE‑2025‑6554 (V8 engine type‑confusion) actively exploited; update to v138.0.7204.96+. Source: https://thehackernews.com/2025/07/google-patches-critical-zero-day-flaw.html
  • CitrixBleed 2 (CVE‑2025‑5777) in NetScaler ADC/Gateway being exploited; immediate patch deployment recommended. Source: https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/citrixbleed-2-exploits-are-now-in-the-wild-so-patch-now

  • DragonForce – ransomware-as-a-service observed in M&S attack. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/business/live/2025/jul/08/stock-markets-trump-tariff-letters-august-deadline-obr-uk-public-finances-marks-spencer-cyber-hack-business-live-news-updates

  • High-profile supply‑chain social engineering (Scattered Spider) underscores need for vetting third‑party and call‑center workflows.
  • Patch critical RCE and zero‑day flaws (SPNEGO, Citrix, Chrome) immediately to mitigate active threats.

U.S.-Focused Threat Intelligence Snapshot

  • No new U.S.-based mega‑incidents, but Scattered Spider operations historically target U.S. airlines and retailers—defenders should monitor IoCs above.
  • Regulatory note: UK is moving toward mandatory breach reporting; U.S. entities may soon face similar trends.

Summary

  • Immediate threats: Scattered Spider + DragonForce ransomware, CitrixBleed‑2, Chrome zero‑day, SPNEGO RCE
  • Actionable steps: Apply Microsoft, Chrome, Citrix, SAP patches; validate third‑party call center controls; deploy IDS rules for CHM/phishing; monitor listed IoCs.
  • Longer‑term needs: Breach reporting frameworks; supply‑chain vetting; third‑party risk governance; intelligence integration via OTX/AbuseIPDB.
