Weekly Brief – August 4, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • AI‑aided ransomware spreads via unpatched Microsoft SharePoint servers; over 400 systems across government, education, healthcare, transportation and finance are reportedly compromised by the Warlock gang. Source: https://www.axios.com/2025/07/29/microsoft-sharepoint-hacks-ransomware
  • Scattered Spider group now deploying DragonForce ransomware to encrypt VMware ESXi servers, shifting from exfiltration to full-scale encryption campaigns. Source: https://cyble.com/blog/scattered-spider-ransomware-in-2025-cyber-alert/
  • St. Paul, Minnesota activates National Guard cyber units after a coordinated breach disrupting city services; parallels with Medusa ransomware cases, investigation ongoing. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/jul/30/tim-walz-minnesota-national-guard
  • Wendy’s confirms PoS malware infected 300 stores, leading to credit card data theft. Source: https://www.trendmicro.com/vinfo/us/security/special-report/data-breach/latest-incidents
  • Educational sector breach: Ravenshaw University website and higher‑education Instagram accounts hacked; unauthorized content posted, quickly remediated. Source: https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/bhubaneswar/instagram-account-of-higher-edu-dept-ravenshaw-website-hacked-later-restored/articleshow/122959538.cms

  • Asus releases updates for MyAsus, Armoury Crate, DriverHub and router models addressing CVE‑2025‑4569 (CVSS 7.7) and CVE‑2025‑4570 (CVSS 6.9); firmware now available via MyAsus app/Microsoft Store.   • Cisco Identity Services Engine (ISE) RCE flaws now under active exploitation. Admins urged to apply patches immediately. Source: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/tag/vulnerability/

Source: https://www.nelsonmullins.com/insights/alerts/privacy_and_data_security_alert/all/privacy-and-data-security-alert-california-finalizes-ccpa-regulation-amendments-new-compliance-obligations-for-cybersecurity-risk-assessments-and-automated-decision-making

  • Trump administration’s revised Executive Order adjusts cybersecurity policy: narrows cyber sanctions to foreign actors only, updating EO 14144 and EO 13694. Source: https://natlawreview.com/article/trumps-new-cybersecurity-executive-order-what-contractors-need-know

Threat Actors

  • Warlock: recently identified ransomware gang exploiting SharePoint zero‑day to attack diverse sectors.
  • Scattered Spider: now deploying DragonForce ransomware, actively encrypting ESXi servers.
  • Volt Typhoon: Chinese state-sponsored actor continuing surveillance campaigns into U.S. critical infrastructure.
  • Storm‑2372: active device code phishing campaign targeting organizations via OAuth phishing.

  • AI‑powered attacks are multiplying TTP sophistication: defenders must accelerate patching cycles and monitor defenses at machine speeds.

  • Ransomware now targeting city incident response teams intensifies threat to critical infrastructure operations.
  • Expanded CCPA regulations could affect organizations nationwide, not just in California, with enhanced audit and compliance expectations.
