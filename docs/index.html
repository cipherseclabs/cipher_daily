Weekly Brief – September 4, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) cyberattack: A sophisticated cyberattack forced JLR to shut down manufacturing and IT systems, disrupting operations (particularly at Halewood and Solihull) but no customer data appears to have been compromised. Source: https://www.itpro.com
  • Hellcat/Scattered Spider Link: Hacker "Rey", linked to Hellcat and tied to Scattered Spider and ShinyHunters, claimed responsibility for the JLR attack via a Telegram channel. Source: https://www.ft.com
  • Salesforce–Salesloft Drift breach: Attackers accessed customer data from Cloudflare, Palo Alto Networks, and Zscaler by compromising third-party application tokens. Source: https://www.cybersecuritydive.com
  • Expanded data theft: Hundreds of incidents continue to emerge following the same supply chain compromise. Source: https://securityboulevard.com
  • Brazil Fintech attack: A $130M heist via Pix payment rails was attempted against Sinqia S.A. and client firms. Source: https://social.cyware.com

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Android: September update patched 111–120 flaws including two exploited zero-days (CVE-2025-38352, CVE-2025-48543). Source: https://www.bitdefender.com
  • Samsung: September 2025 SMR fixes 86 vulnerabilities across Galaxy devices. Source: https://sammyguru.com
  • WhatsApp (Meta): Patched CVE-2025-55177, a zero-click exploit on iOS/macOS targeting VIPs. Source: https://www.techradar.com
  • Chrome: Fixed high-severity CVE-2025-9864 (use-after-free in V8 engine). Source: https://www.securityweek.com
  • Django: Patched high-severity SQL injection flaw in latest release. Source: https://cyberpress.org

Geopolitical & Political Developments

  • FEMA breach fallout: 24 IT staff fired following credential reuse issues; possible Chinese actor involvement. Source: https://www.axios.com
  • MS-ISAC budget cuts: DHS to end $27M support by Sept 30, affecting cybersecurity for 19,000+ local entities. Source: https://www.axios.com

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • TP-Link flaws: CISA added two active TP-Link auth flaws to KEV catalog. Source: https://gbhackers.com
  • Microsoft SharePoint (ToolShell): Patched CVE-2025-49704, -49706, and bypasses (CVE-2025-53770, -53771). Webshells + ransomware usage observed. Source: https://www.cisa.gov

Threat Actors

  • "Rey"/Hellcat/Scattered Spider: Attributed to the JLR attack. Same actor tied to M&S and ShinyHunters campaigns. Source: https://www.theguardian.com
  • Unknown actor: Used third-party Drift/Salesloft access to breach multiple vendor-connected Salesforce environments. Source: https://www.cybersecuritydive.com

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

  • JLR response: Demonstrates best-practice containment but reflects growing risks to industrial control operations.
  • Supply chain risk: Requires improved monitoring, faster credential revocation, and vetting of third-party integrations.
  • Patch urgency: Prioritize Android, WhatsApp, and SharePoint CVEs due to active exploitation.

U.S.-Focused Threat Intelligence Snapshot

  • MS-ISAC risk: Funding cut could paralyze local incident response and information sharing. Source: https://www.axios.com

Summary

Immediate threats
  • Active zero-day exploitation in Android and WhatsApp
  • JLR operational outage with potential cascading industry impact
  • Widening Salesforce–Salesloft supply chain breach
Actionable steps
  • Prioritize patching for Android, WhatsApp, Chrome, and SharePoint
  • Harden third-party app integrations and revoke unused OAuth tokens
  • Review IR plans for manufacturing/industrial downtime scenarios
Longer-term needs

  • Legislative support for MS-ISAC continuation
  • Invest in zero-trust policy enforcement across federal agencies
