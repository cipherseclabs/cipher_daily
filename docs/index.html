Daily Brief – July 10, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • UK National Crime Agency arrested four individuals (ages 17–20) linked to ransomware incidents in April targeting Marks & Spencer, Co‑Op, and Harrods. Estimated £300 M losses; attackers (“Scattered Spider” & “DragonForce”) used SIM‑swap, phishing, help‑desk impersonation, and DragonForce encryption software. Source: https://apnews.com/article/9f9ea474a42acd147b81c5a7ff3ff05d
  • Qantas breach on July 2 impacted ~6 M customers via third‑party call center; names, emails, phones, birth dates stolen—but no financial or passport data. Investigated by Australian Federal Police; parallels drawn to Scattered Spider tactics. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/jul/07/qantas-contacted-by-suspected-cyber-criminal-but-airline-wont-confirm-if-hacking-ransom-demanded
  • IT supplier Ingram Micro hit by SafePay ransomware, disrupting order fulfillment. Source: https://www.axios.com/newsletters/axios-future-of-cybersecurity-fab0c0d0-5760-11f0-8c09-e72f7ef7d797
  • Mid‑June UNFI (food distributor) attack caused regional grocery shortages—attack origin attributed to unauthorized system access. Source: https://insights.integrity360.com/5-of-the-biggest-cyber-attacks-of-2025-so-far

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Fortinet report: 52% of organizations now assign OT security under CISOs. OT security maturity (visibility, segmentation) has increased—26% at Level 1, majority at Level 2. Source: https://industrialcyber.co/reports/fortinet-finds-ot-security-maturity-reduces-attacks-as-more-cisos-are-at-the-helm-in-2025/
  • Fortinet released critical patch for FortiWeb: CVE‑2025‑25257 (SQL injection, CVSS 9.6) allowing unauthenticated remote code execution. Source: https://securityonline.info/fortinet-fixes-critical-sql-injection-flaw-in-fortiweb-cve-2025-25257-cvss-9-6/

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • Microsoft July Patch Tuesday addressed 130 CVEs (including 10 critical); resolved publicly disclosed SQL Server flaw CVE‑2025‑49719 (info leak, CVSS 7.5), RCE in SPNEGO/SharePoint/Kerberos, Office use‑after‑free CVEs. No new zero‑days. Source: https://thehackernews.com/2025/07/microsoft-patches-130-vulnerabilities.html
  • Fortinet zero‑day CVE‑2025‑32756 (arbitrary code exec) exploited in the wild; affects FortiVoice, FortiMail, FortiNDR, FortiRecorder, FortiCamera (CVSS 9.6). Source: https://www.tenable.com/blog/cve-2025-32756-zero-day-vulnerability-in-multiple-fortinet-products-exploited-in-the-wild

Geopolitical & Political Developments
