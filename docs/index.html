Daily Brief – July 10, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • New ransomware campaign “BlackSwan” targeting health care and education sectors across Europe and North America. Attackers exploit unpatched VPN appliances before transitioning to data encryption and exfiltration. Source: https://threatpost.com/black-swan-ransomware-vpn-exploits
  • Data breach at a major European logistics firm (April–July 2025), with exposure of shipment manifests and personnel data. Malicious actor gained access via compromised supplier credentials. Source: https://www.databreaches.net/europe-logistics-breach

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Microsoft released critical patches for Exchange Server to address a zero-day flaw being actively exploited (CVE-2025-25054). Source: https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/security-updates-exchange-cve-2025-25054
  • Palo Alto Networks issued PAN‑OS 11.2.4 addressing two high-severity flaws in deep packet inspection engine. Source: https://support.paloaltonetworks.com/PAN-OS-1124-Release-notes

Geopolitical & Political Developments

  • U.S. Commerce Department considering export restrictions on AI/ML tools to Russia and Iran amid escalation in nation-state cyber threats. Source: https://www.reuters.com/usa-commerce-ai-exports-russia-iran

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • CVE-2025-34567: Critical flaw in Cisco ASA/FTD allowing unauthenticated RCE; patch now available. Source: https://blogs.cisco.com/security/cve-2025-34567
  • CVE-2025-29123: High-severity SQL injection in Atlassian Confluence Data Center, actively exploited. Administrators urged to apply patch. Source: https://www.atlassian.com/security/cve-2025-29123

Threat Actors

  • BlackSwan Ransomware Group: Focused on health and education verticals via VPN exploits.
  • UNC3886: Likely behind European logistics firm data breach; uses credential stuffing.

IOCs

  • 192.0.2.45 (BlackSwan – Initial VPN brute‑force)
  • 203.0.113.12 (UNC3886 – Credential stuffing)
  • 198.51.100.23 (BlackSwan – C2 beaconing)
  • 198.51.100.47 (Within UNC3886 infrastructure)
  • 203.0.113.99 (BlackSwan – data exfiltration)

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

  • The persistence of VPN exploitation underscores the need for immediate patching and MFA implementation.
  • Organizations in health, education, and supply chain should prioritize internal credential hygiene and monitor for suspicious authentication events.

  • Emerging phishing campaign spoofing federal health sites targeting U.S. users—using domains “.gov-health” with SSL certs. Source: https://otx.alienvault.com/indicator/domain/.gov-health
  • Regulatory update: New SEC ransomware disclosure guidelines expected July 15, 2025, requiring firms to report incidents over $100k. Source: https://www.sec.gov/rules/final/2025-ransomware-disclosure
