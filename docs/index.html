Weekly Brief – July 16, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • Chinese-affiliated threat actors (Salt Typhoon, Volt Typhoon, Silk Typhoon) ramped up cyber-espionage against U.S. and allied targets in 2024–2025, exploiting zero-days and private-sector “hacking-for-hire” contractors. Silk Typhoon infected ~100 victims, including media organizations, and is known for advanced stealth TTPs. Source: https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2025/07/16/china-hacking-us-targets/
  • Ingram Micro is recovering from a global ransomware attack by the SafePay gang via GlobalProtect VPN. The firm adopted containment measures and resumed operations across multiple regions, but some areas remain affected. Source: https://www.itpro.com/security/cyber-attacks/ingram-micro-cyber-attack-it-distributor-says-system-restoration-underway-but-some-customers-might-have-to-wait-for-a-return-to-normality
  • UK law enforcement arrested four individuals (ages 17–20) allegedly linked to ransomware/blackmail campaigns impacting Marks & Spencer, Co‑op, and Harrods. Investigators are exploring connections to Scattered Spider and DragonForce. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2025/jul/10/four-arrested-over-cyber-attacks-marks-and-spencer-co-op-harrods
  • A 13‑year-old critical remote‑brakes hijacking vulnerability in U.S. train telemetry modules (CVE‑2025‑1727) was confirmed, prompting a CISA advisory and commitments for fixes by 2027. Source: https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/cyber-security/security-vulnerability-on-u-s-trains-that-let-anyone-activate-the-brakes-on-the-rear-car-was-known-for-13-years-operators-refused-to-fix-the-issue-until-now

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Google released Chrome 138.0.7204.157/.158 to patch critical zero-day CVE‑2025‑6558 (GPU/ANGLE sandbox escape), confirmed exploited in the wild. Similar updates issued for Edge, Brave, etc. Source: https://thehackernews.com/2025/07/urgent-google-releases-critical-chrome.html
  • Microsoft July Patch Tuesday addressed 130–137 CVEs (130 per SecurityWeek, 137 per CrowdStrike), including a wormable Windows RCE (CVE‑2025‑47981), SQL Server info‑leak zero‑day (CVE‑2025‑49719), and several RCEs in SPNEGO, SharePoint, Kerberos KDC. Source: https://www.securityweek.com/microsoft-patches-130-vulnerabilities-for-july-2025-patch-tuesday/
  • Citrix urged patching of CVE‑2025‑5777 (“CitrixBleed 2”), actively exploited in NetScaler ADC/Gateway appliances, with CISA mandating federal patching within 24 hours. Source: https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/cisa-warns-hackers-are-actively-exploiting-critical-citrixbleed-2
  • Binarly disclosed four high-severity UEFI/SMM vulnerabilities on Gigabyte motherboards (CVE‑2025‑7026–7029), allowing potential firmware compromise and Secure Boot bypass; firmware updates already available. Source: https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/motherboards/critical-uefi-vulnerabilities-found-in-gigabyte-motherboards-allow-attackers-to-bypass-secure-boot-and-install-firmware-backdoors

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

  • State-backed espionage via contracted cyber-for-hire services continues to blur the line between espionage and criminality—requires enhanced supply‑chain visibility.
  • CitrixBleed 2 and Chrome GPU zero-days highlight critical need for accelerated patch cadence across perimeter and endpoint stacks.
  • Firmware-level vulnerabilities (e.g., Gigabyte) represent a growing low-detection risk vector—advocate UEFI monitoring and immutability enforcement.
